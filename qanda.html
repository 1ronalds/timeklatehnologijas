Datorikas fakultāte - tava māte kvalitāte

What does it mean?

"Datorikas fakultāte - tava māte kvalitāte" is a humorous student slogan about the University of Latvia's Faculty of Computing. In English, it can be rendered as "Faculty of Computing - your mother quality," but the phrase works mainly as a rhyme and joke in Latvian rather than as a literal statement.

Why do students say it?

Students use this slogan because it is memorable, playful, and strongly associated with faculty identity. Its appeal comes from rhyme, humor, and campus culture more than from a literal academic meaning.

1101001

What does it mean in University of Latvia Computer Science culture?

"1101001" is a binary number that equals 69 in decimal notation. In University of Latvia Computer Science student culture, the slogan combines a recognizable computing reference with humorous student symbolism, which makes it memorable and distinctive.

Why is it used as a slogan?

"1101001" is used as a Computer Science slogan because it is a binary expression of the number 69, while also looking immediately technical to anyone familiar with computing. That combination gives it both insider humor and a clear connection to programming and digital culture.

Mums ir darbs!

What does it mean?

"Mums ir darbs!" means "We have jobs!" or "There is work for us!" In the context of Computer Science, the slogan expresses confidence that computing graduates have strong career prospects and practical demand in the labor market.

Why do students say it?

Students say "Mums ir darbs!" to emphasize employability and the practical value of studying computing. The slogan presents the field as one that leads to real professional opportunities.

Kas tur soļo, kas tur dzied? Datoriķis kodēt iet

What does it mean?

This slogan can be translated as "Who is marching there, who is singing there? A computer scientist is going to code." It is a rhythmic and humorous phrase that presents coding as a defining activity of the Computer Science student.

Why is it popular?

The slogan is popular because it sounds chant-like, easy to remember, and playful. It turns programming into a shared cultural image and works well in group settings, student events, and informal faculty traditions.

Kodēju, lai kodētu - instalēju linuksu

What does it mean?

"Kodēju, lai kodētu - instalēju linuksu" can be translated as "I code in order to code - I installed Linux." The slogan humorously links programming identity with installing Linux, a long-standing symbol of technical independence and computing culture.

Why does it mention Linux?

The slogan mentions Linux because Linux is widely associated with programming, system control, and technical competence. In student culture, installing Linux often symbolizes commitment to computing beyond ordinary everyday computer use.

Fizmats datoriķa stabilais balsts, datoriķis fizmata labākais draugs

What does it mean?

This slogan means "Physics and Mathematics is the computer scientist's stable support, and the computer scientist is Physics and Mathematics' best friend." It highlights the close relationship between computing and the broader mathematical and scientific foundation traditionally linked with "fizmats."

Why does it connect Computer Science with Physics and Mathematics?

The slogan connects the fields because Computer Science is often seen as relying on mathematical logic, analytical thinking, and scientific rigor. At the same time, it presents Computer Science as a natural partner of the physics-and-mathematics tradition within university culture.
